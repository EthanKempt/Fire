Rules

Higley Assassins is a continuous competition organized by students and for students at Higley. During the game, students organize into teams and play the roles of assassins, targeting other students in attempts to eliminate them. The final team with any players not eliminated are the winners of the game and the prize money.

Juniors and Seniors must form teams of 3 or 4 students and pay the entry fee of [DOLLARS] before the deadline of [DATE] to be registered to play. The entire team must be paid for at one time. When paying the entry fee, teams must also communicate their team name and their members with the directors. Payment can be taken in cash, with written team details attached, or through Venmo to the account [ACCOUNT], with team details in the transaction description.

Barring [Variation Weeks], teams are each secretly assigned a target team through Instagram [LINK TO INSTAGRAM PAGE] and their team page on HigleyAssassins.com [LINK] every week. To eliminate a member of their target team, they must get their targets wet using water in any method they choose, then give video evidence to the directors through Instagram [LINK]. For an elimination to be official, the assassins must not have broken any other rules in the process and have easily verifiable proof that the target was caught. Official eliminations will be posted publicly on Instagram [LINK]. A team’s failure to eliminate a member of their target team in a given week will result in their own elimination. For determining a team’s standing with respect to this rule, evidence of the elimination of a target must be sent to the directors prior to 6:00 p.m., Sunday. At or very soon after this point, teams will receive their next assignment and the next week will begin.

A number of places are Safe Zones, where players cannot be eliminated. These include and are limited to:
  • Higley Campus, including parking lots, sports fields, and, during the player’s scheduled school hours, the Seminary church and space between it and the main campus.
  • Williams Field Campus, including parking lots, sports fields, and the pool.
  • A player’s own home interior. This does not apply if invited in by a resident. This intentionally does not include yards.
  • A player’s own workplace. This only applies while the student is currently clocked in and working a shift, including working outside the building (such as taking out trash or walking to a delivery driving car), although it intentionally does not apply while they are walking into or out of work before or after their shift, even if they tried to clock in from their car.
  • Venues where players attend athletic or other programs specifically run by Higley High School, including practices, games, performances, etc. This safety applies only to students participating or involved in the Higley program, and only during the times which they occur. Parking lots are included.
  • Venues where religious activities take place, including parking lots or, in the case of activities at a home or other small building, the yard and general surroundings.

There will be limited opportunities for eliminated players to buy back into the game, meaning they will be immediately reinstated as active players upon paying. The price of buy backs and the circumstances in which they will be available are up to the discretion of the directors and may not be announced prior to their availability. If a player buys back, the team that is currently assigned to hunt them will be notified.

There may be announcements made by the directors of bonus challenges with associated rewards. These will typically involve a deadline by which teams must complete the challenge or end once one team completes it. Specific details for each bonus challenge and its reward will be announced together. There will be no punishments for non-completion of bonus challenges.

There may be variation weeks announced, during which the typical flow of Assassins will be intentionally altered to provide additional novelty during the week. Critical gameplay elements will not be altered. Any rules that will be removed, added, or adjusted during variation weeks will be announced on Instagram [LINK] and HigleyAssassins.com [LINK] along with the time period for which the ruleset will be altered.

Prize money accumulates in the pot when players pay the entry fee or buy back. At any time during the game, the current pot value will be public on HigleyAssassins.com [LINK]. The directors may each take a small cut of the money, not any more than [DOLLARS]. Additionally, maintenance costs for the website will be sourced from the pot money. All information on monetary history will always be public on HigleyAssassins.com. The remaining money is then delivered to the winning team at their convenience. In the case of all remaining teams simultaneously being eliminated or multiple teams remaining by the end of the school year, they will all be considered the winners and the money will be equally distributed between the teams.

  • This Ruleset is intended to be comprehensive, detailed, and clear. A particular situation or action not specifically outlined in or following from a common sense reading of these rules is generally permitted, though, ultimately, the directors will collectively decide on matters where the rules may be vague or ambiguous, then reclarify the rules to reflect our decision.

  • In the case of a rule change, announcements will be made on both Instagram [LINK] and on HigleyAssassins.com [LINK].
